Tom Jones net worth-Welsh singer, Sir Thomas Jones Woodward, also known as Tom Jones was born on June 7th, 1940 at 57 Kingsland Terrace in Treforest in Wales.

Jones was born to Freda Jones and Thomas Woodward. He shares the same parents with his sister, Sheila Woodward.

He is predominantly of English ancestry; his Welsh maternal grandfather, Welsh maternal grandmother, Somerset and Wiltshire-born English paternal grandfather, and Wiltshire-born English paternal grandmother were all born in Wales.

He went to Pontypridd Central Secondary Modern School, Wood Road Junior School, and Wood Road Infants School. At a young age, he started singing; he frequently sang in his school choir, at family events, and at weddings.

He disliked athletics and school, but his singing ability helped him become more self-assured. He received a TB diagnosis when he was 12 years old.

Tom Jones career

Jones’s career began with a string of top 10 hits in the 1960s and he has since toured regularly, with appearances in Las Vegas from 1967 to 2011. His voice has been described by AllMusic as a “full-throated, robust baritone”.

Gospel, pop, R&B, show tunes, country, dance, and dance were among the genres Jones has performed. He was described as a “musical chameleon who could slide from deep rasp to pop coo, with a voice as velvety as it was lovely,” by the New York Times in 2008.

His hit songs include “It’s Not Unusual,” “What’s New Pussycat?” (the theme song for the James Bond movie Thunderball from 1965), “Green, Green Grass of Home,” “Delilah,” “She’s a Lady,” “Sex Bomb,” and a cover of Prince’s “Kiss.” He has sold over 100 million records worldwide.

Additionally, Jones has dabbled in acting on occasion, making his screen debut in the lead role of the television movie Pleasure Cove (1979). Additionally, he made a cameo appearance in Tim Burton’s movie Mars Attacks! (1996).

He was nominated for a 1970 Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Television Series Musical or Comedy for his work as the host of the show This Is Tom Jones.

He performed in his first dramatic acting role in a Playhouse Presents episode in 2012. In addition to two Brit Awards, Best British Male in 2000 and Outstanding Contribution to Music in 2003, he also won an MTV Video Music Award in 1989 and a Grammy Award for Best New Artist in 1966.

For his contributions to music, he was awarded an OBE in 1998 and a knighthood by Queen Elizabeth II in 2005. Due to his work as a coach on the talent competition The Voice UK, his popularity increased in the 2010s (2012–present).

The sound of American soul music influenced Jones’ singing technique. Little Richard, Solomon Burke, Jackie Wilson, Brook Benton, Elvis Presley (his hero and subsequently close friend), Jerry Lee Lewis, and other blues, R&B, and rock and roll performers were among his earliest influences.
