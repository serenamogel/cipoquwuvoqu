John Deere designed the G model to be a three-plow tractor with 34HP power at the belt. Power came from a 412.5-cubic-inch, two-cylinder, all-fuel engine that ran on distillates and kerosene.

Famously, the John Deere Model G’s engine was so much larger than the powerplants given in the rest of the company’s lineup that the tractor’s frame had to be customized with the addition of a bulge to provide its girth.
