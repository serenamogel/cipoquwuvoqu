Tara Louise Obituary, Death – Tara Louise (Ford) Haberl, 52, of Robinson, passed away at Baylor Scott & White Hillcrest Medical Center in Waco early Tuesday afternoon, October 31, 2023. On Wednesday, November 8, 2023, at 11:00 a.m., funeral services will be held at the Aderhold Funeral Home in West, presided over by Dr. Cary Killough. Following that, interment will take place at St. Mary’s Cemetery near West. The family will be open to visitors at the funeral home on Tuesday from 6:00 to 8:00 p.m.

Tara was born in Baltimore, Maryland on September 8, 1971. She is the daughter of David Ford and Sharon (Treulieb) Harvison. She graduated from Kenwood High School in Essex, Maryland in 1989 and obtained a Bachelor of Science in Nursing from Mary Hardin Baylor University in Belton, Texas in 1993. In Waco on June 12, 1993, she entered into matrimony with Paul Haberl. Tara belonged to the Waco congregation of Meadowbrook Baptist Church. Additionally, she held memberships in the Robinson Education Foundation and the Robinson Band Boosters. Her tenure as a certified registered nurse surpassed three decades.

Tara most recently held the position of nurse home visitor at Hillcrest’s Nurse Family Partnership. She would advocate for and educate mothers and their children throughout Central Texas during her time there. She devised a multitude of innovations aimed at enhancing the readiness of first-time mothers. Tara demonstrated her extraordinary creativity through quilting, scrapbooking, chalk painting, and the creation of T-shirts, flasks, and other items for her family. She delighted in visiting Disney and Universal’s Harry Potter attractions and viewings. She enjoyed camping, decorating throughout the year, and sharing Sunday dinners with friends and family.

Tara was predeceased by her father and father-in-law, David Ford Sr. and Malcolm Haberl, respectively. Her cherished spouse of thirty years, Paul Haberl of Robinson, Doris Haberl, her mother, Sharon Harvison and her husband Alton, her children Madison Haberl, Meredith Haberl, Makaelin Haberl and fiance Baylor Medlin, and Max Haberl, her sister Carrie Copenhaver and her husband Randy, her brother David Ford Jr. and his wife Susanne, and her sister-in-law Susan Darling and her husband Jason are also survivors.
