Music Career, Alabama Band and Farms

Teddy Gentry was in high school when he got interested in music and began focusing on his passion. In 1969, he formed a band alongside his cousin named Randy Owen. Initially, the band was named ‘Wild Country’ but was later changed to ‘Alabama’. Later, the band was also joined by their cousin Jeff Cook. Teddy is a bassist and vocalist of the group.

The group started professionally in 1972 and started writing original songs as well. Their debut ‘I Want to Be with You Tonight’ was a super hit and became 78 on the Billboard Hot County Songs charts. Afterward, they started releasing many songs back to back. The group has signed the contract with various recording labels named MDJ, RCA Nashville, BMG Chrysalis, GRT, and others.

In 2019, the band also won the first-ever Life Time Achievement Award. Not only this, but Teddy Gentry also owns a farm named Bent Tree Farms. He bought the farm of his grandfather of 60 acres and his great-grandfather’s neighborhood farm of 80 acres. As per this, he owns a total farm size of around 140 acres.
